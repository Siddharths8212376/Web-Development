Carl August Nielsen (Danish: [kɑːl ˈnelsn̩]; 9 June 1865 – 3 October 1931) was a Danish musician, conductor and violinist, widely recognized as his country's most prominent composer. Brought up by poor but musically talented parents on the island of Funen, he demonstrated his musical abilities at an early age.

He initially played in a military band before attending the Royal Danish Academy of Music in Copenhagen from 1884 until December 1886. He premiered his Op. 1, Suite for Strings, in 1888, at the age of 23. The following year, Nielsen began a 16-year stint as a second violinist in the Royal Danish Orchestra under the conductor Johan Svendsen, during which he played in Giuseppe Verdi's Falstaff and Otello at their Danish premieres. In 1916, he took a post teaching at the Royal Academy and continued to work there until his death. Although his symphonies, concertos and choral music are now internationally acclaimed, Nielsen's career and personal life were marked by many difficulties, often reflected in his music.

13 Reasons Why (stylized onscreen as TH1RTEEN R3ASONS WHY) is an American narrative drama based on the 2007 novel Thirteen Reasons Why by Jay Asher. Originally earmarked as a feature film, it was instead adapted by Brian Yorkey for the global streaming network Netflix as a webcast television series. The central characters are a seventeen year old high school student, Clay Jensen, and his deceased female friend Hannah Baker, who has committed suicide after failing to cope with the culture, gossip and lack of support from her friends and her school. A box of cassette tapes recorded by Hannah in the lead up to her suicide details thirteen reasons why she ended her life. The original series and its subsequent second series provides a critical overview of American high school culture with an emphasis on the marginalised, less athletic youth who appear to endure sanctioned repression as part of their educational experience. As a global, streaming network release, the program highlights the unique aspects of the American high school system to the broader English-speaking audience worldwide via the contemporary streaming systems more popular among modern youth.
